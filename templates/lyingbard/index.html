His Tale of Woe

The Lying Bard, as he is so cruely named, is a vagrant of unfortunate circumstance. He is an entertainer by trade, but with society as it is there is no place for him in the royal courts. Thus he is cast onto the streets where he wanders and performs. While many delight in his impersonations, few will provide for him.

 Alas he commands no attention from the thousandaires and heiresses of uptown. So, for now, he is confined to a life of destitution.

 His quarters are unbecoming of him and have lead him to a life of sluggish pace and poor countenance. He dreams of a chateau in the clouds costing not more than $100 a month. From there he would be as spry as the day he was born and lead a life of enrapturing entertainment for all to enjoy.
